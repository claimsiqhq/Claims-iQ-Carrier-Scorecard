### 1. Diagnostic Screen OCR (The "Undeniable Proof" Standard)
Top adjusters don't just photograph a water stain; they photograph the screens of their diagnostic tools to prove the extent of the damage. Your AI must use Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to read the actual numbers on these devices:
*   **Moisture Meters:** In the Farrell and Mattson claims, the adjuster uses a Tramex ME5 moisture meter. Your AI must detect the device and read the screen to verify the severity, such as reading **"18.8 WOOD"** [2], **"91.0 DRYWALL"**, or **"100.0 DRYWALL"** [3, 4].

*   *Accuracy Check:* If the estimate includes tearing out drywall or setting up dehumidifiers, the AI *must* find these exact tool readings in the photo sheet to justify the cost.

### 2. Laser Measurement Verification
To ensure the Xactimate sketch perfectly matches the photos, the AI needs to validate the dimensional proof.
*   In the provided files, the adjuster uses Bosch Professional GLM 20 and Leica DISTO D1 laser measures to justify the sizes of windows and rooms [7-9]. 
*   *Accuracy Check:* The AI should extract the measurements from the laser screens (e.g., reading 11' 0 3/16" [9]) or physical tape measures [10] and cross-reference them against the dimensions listed in the sketch and estimate.

### 3. Sequential Label Alignment
The carrier explicitly deducts points if the photos are out of order [1]. The AI must read the burned-in file paths at the top of every photo block.
*   For example, it must verify that photos labeled `Exterior (elevations)/Front` [11] strictly precede interior photos labeled `Interior/Upper level/Living Room / Kitchen` [12].
*   It must also ensure the visual damage matches the adjuster's caption. If the caption says "Floor - gapping and mouthing at joints" [13] or "Water saturated bookshelf" [14], the AI must confirm those specific damages are clearly visible in the frame.
