==================================================
DESK ADJUSTER SCORECARD REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Implement the following DA categories and scoring logic based on the provided standards:

1) File Stack Order
Weight:
- 10 points normally
- 15 points if denial-letter category is not applicable

Definition:
The stack order should flow as:
- DA report
- Statement of Loss (SOL)
- Payment Letter
- Other Letters
- Estimate
- Photos
- Sketch
- Prior Loss Report

Question key:
- is_file_stack_order_correct

Pass criteria:
- documents are arranged in a logical and expected order
- ordering improves desk review flow

Partial criteria:
- mostly logical but missing one or two expected placements

Fail criteria:
- materially disorganized or illogical sequence

2) Payment Recommendations Match
Weight:
- 20 points normally
- 25 points if denial-letter category is not applicable

Definition:
Payment figures on:
- DA report
- SOL
- Payment letter
must all agree, and deductible must be correctly applied.

Question keys:
- do_payment_values_match
- is_deductible_correct

Pass criteria:
- all key payment values align
- deductible correctly applied
- no unexplained discrepancies

Partial criteria:
- minor discrepancy or unclear explanation, but likely reconcilable

Fail criteria:
- material mismatch in payment values
- deductible incorrect
- unexplained inconsistency

3) DA Report Quality
Weight:
- 10 points normally
- 15 points if denial-letter category is not applicable

Definition:
The DA report should summarize clearly and should not copy/paste too much from the FA report.
The FA report tells the detailed story. The DA report should be a concise summary with recommendations.

Question key:
- is_da_report_concise_and_decisive

Pass criteria:
- concise
- recommendation-focused
- not redundant
- clear disposition

Partial criteria:
- understandable but too long, repetitive, or vague

Fail criteria:
- bloated, copy-paste heavy, unclear recommendation, or missing concise summary

4) Unique Policy Provisions Addressed
Weight:
- 25 points normally
- 30 points if denial-letter category is not applicable

Definition:
The desk review should address policy-specific provisions where relevant.
Examples in the standards include:
- HO6 master policy included
- landlord living in the home requires municipal lien certificate mention
- HSB covered items not included in estimate
- sublimits or endorsements that affect payment
- commercial or form-specific provisions if applicable

Question key:
- are_unique_policy_provisions_addressed

Pass criteria:
- claim-specific policy provisions, endorsements, forms, sublimits, exclusions, and special handling items are actually recognized and discussed where relevant

Partial criteria:
- policy reviewed generally but important claim-specific provision not fully tied to claim outcome

Fail criteria:
- relevant special provision exists but is not recognized or addressed

5) Prior Losses Addressed
Weight:
- 10 points normally
- 15 points if denial-letter category is not applicable

Definition:
The stack should include prior loss / ISO reporting at the end, and priors within the past 5 years should be reviewed and either:
- noted as not relevant
or
- noted as relevant and further requested/investigated

Question key:
- are_prior_losses_addressed

Pass criteria:
- prior losses reviewed and disposition stated

Partial criteria:
- prior loss material present but treatment not fully explained

Fail criteria:
- prior losses exist or ISO report expected and not addressed

6) Denial Letters Correct
Weight:
- 25 points if applicable
- if no denial applies, redistribute by using the “score if no denials exist” values above

Definition:
If denial letters are applicable, the denial letter must cite the correct policy language and correct reason.
Example from the standard:
- if flood waters entered the building, cite surface water exclusion, not wear and tear

Question key:
- is_denial_letter_correct

Applicability logic:
- This question is only applicable if the claim includes a denial or partial denial requiring denial language

Pass criteria:
- correct denial rationale
- correct policy language
- causation and exclusion match facts

Partial criteria:
- mostly correct but language or rationale needs tightening

Fail criteria:
- incorrect exclusion cited
- wrong policy rationale
- denial logic inconsistent with facts

==================================================
FIELD ADJUSTER SCORECARD REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Implement the following FA categories and scoring logic:

1) Estimate Operational Order
Weight:
- 20 points

Definition:
Estimate should follow a logical operational order, such as:
- roof/exterior before interior where applicable
- floors, walls, ceilings, accessories in logical sequence
- debris removal near the end
- line items grouped sensibly by area/trade/process

Question key:
- is_estimate_in_operational_order

Pass criteria:
- estimate reads in logical repair flow and supports review efficiency

Partial criteria:
- mostly logical but somewhat scattered

Fail criteria:
- estimate sequencing materially disorganized

2) Photographs Clear and In Order
Weight:
- 20 points

Definition:
Photo labels, room order, and image sequence should generally follow the estimate flow.
Photo quality should be clear and usable.

Question key:
- are_photographs_clear_and_in_order

Pass criteria:
- clear images
- usable documentation
- labeling/ordering generally follows estimate flow

Partial criteria:
- some photos weak, missing, out of order, or poorly labeled

Fail criteria:
- poor quality, inadequate support, or major sequencing/labelling problems

3) FA Report Quality
Weight:
- 30 points

Definition:
The FA report should adequately describe what is seen in photos and written into the estimate and should correctly address:
- observed damage
- estimate support
- coverage concerns
- subrogation concerns where relevant
- cause and scope consistency

Question key:
- does_fa_report_support_estimate_and_scope

Pass criteria:
- FA narrative clearly supports estimate and documentation

Partial criteria:
- generally supports estimate but has gaps in explanation

Fail criteria:
- estimate unsupported by FA narrative or material mismatch exists

4) Unique Policy Provisions Addressed
Weight:
- 30 points

Definition:
FA handling should reflect policy provisions and sublimits where relevant.
Examples in the standards:
- tree debris limit
- water backup limit
- no storm-created opening
- sublimits
- endorsements
- claim-specific special policy triggers

Question key:
- does_fa_address_unique_policy_provisions

Pass criteria:
- field report demonstrates awareness of relevant policy-related constraints or triggers

Partial criteria:
- provision likely relevant but incompletely handled

Fail criteria:
- relevant provision materially ignored

SCORING RULES
==================================================

1) Each DA and FA scorecard must total to 100 points.
2) If DA denial-letter category is not applicable, reweight DA categories exactly as follows:
- File Stack Order = 15
- Payment Recommendations Match = 25
- DA Report Quality = 15
- Unique Policy Provisions Addressed = 30
- Prior Losses Addressed = 15
- Denial Letters Correct = omitted as N/A
3) Partial credit is allowed.
4) Each question must return:
- status: pass | partial | fail | not_applicable
- points_awarded
- max_points
- issue
- impact
- fix
- evidence_locations
5) Category subtotal must be computed from the questions inside that category.
6) DA percentage, FA percentage, and combined overall percentage must be returned.
7) Combined overall score can be a weighted blend of DA and FA. Default to 50/50 unless configuration exists.
8) Readiness label should be derived from overall score:
- 90–100 = READY
- 75–89 = REVIEW
- below 75 = NOT READY
9) Technical risk label should be derived from issue severity:
- LOW
- MEDIUM
- HIGH
10) Summary must explicitly count:
- failed questions
- partial-credit questions
- passed questions
- warnings
